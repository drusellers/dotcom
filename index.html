Hi, I'm Dru Sellers. I am a problem solver. I build solutions that solve the right problem, and provide enduring value.

I am a software developer who wants more from the language writers, the framework builders and application makers of the world. Software development is still too hard. There is just so much to learn in the realm of the business for us to have to deal with shitty error messages, stupid syntax errors, and flakey doodleware. I have been told that I am not happy with anything in world of code. This is true, I expect more.

I seek out people better than me and I listen. I make it, I break it and I try to understand it. Throw away code is my best friend. I ask for help, and feel humbled by my peers. Again, I listen to my peers, through them I have been able to gain huge amounts of knowledge in a very short periods of time. I argue with them, and philosophize with them. I fight for my beliefs and I change my mind. I am better because of this.

It continues to amaze me the number of ways we can skin any problem solvable through software. It blows me away that whenever I think I have the magic hammer, someone will stand up and show me a better way. Thank you.

I get frustrated when I can't articulate to the business why something is 'wrong.' I get frustrated because I don't have the language to convey the cost of 'wrong', it 'works' after all, right? So, I search for ways to explain the difference between 'works' and 'correct'.

-d
